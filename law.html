Law, Politics, & Terrorism

Law: What it is.

- The difference between crime and moral wrong.

The difference between crime and moral wrong is what kind of harm was caused, and if the consequence of the action was fair.

- The nature of crime.

Generally speaking, the nature of crime is usually of ill-intent. Usually, the crime committed will harm another person in an unfair way.

- Uses of technology that aren't illegal but should be.

There are uses of technology that have very big gray areas of they cause harm to an individual or not. Some topics like piracy can be hard to convince people of it being a crime, because there isn't a direct cause-effect relationship of downloading something without permission.
